Who it's for

Built for the team that will sit in the MFS project.

This is for you if…

  • Your warehouse connects EWM to physical automation - conveyors, cranes, sorters, shuttles via PLC.
  • You run an in-house SAP EWM team facing your first - or next - MFS project.
  • You're a project or IT lead who needs the team ready before kickoff, not learning on the project's clock.
  • You want to reduce dependency on expensive external resources for the parts your own people can own.
  • You're a developer or consultant looking to build solid, practical knowledge of EWM MFS.

It's not the right fit if…

  • You're after a general EWM introduction. This is MFS-specific, end to end.
  • There's no automation in scope of your project. Without an MFS layer, most of this won't apply.
  • You want a certificate rather than project readiness. The goal here is a team that performs in the project - not a badge to hand on the wall.
Best timing: before project start or during the design / blueprint phase - while decisions are still cheap to change, and new knowledge can be applied immediately rather than shelved until later.
The training

Three components that build on each other - from the standard, to its extensions up to running a real project.

Interactive throughout, with the emulation & visualization running alongside most of the sessions to make the concepts stay concrete and practical.

01

MFS Basics

How SAP EWM actually controls physical automation — the foundation everything else depends on.

  • MFS architecture and how it communicates with the PLC layer
  • Important objects - Communication points, resources, queues and many more
  • Different types of subsystems that MFS can control
  • Telegrams and the EWM ↔ PLC handshake
  • Putaway and stock removal strategies
  • Monitoring material flow in the warehouse management monitor
02

Enhancing the standard functionality

Where the MFS standard ends and your project's real requirements begin - how to extend without painting yourself into a corner.

  • Recognising the capabilities and the limits of standard MFS before you build around them
  • Common enhancement points: telegram structures, telegram processing, exception logic and so on
  • BAdIs and the safe places to extend
  • When not to enhance - using the standard on purpose
03

Preparation for MFS projects

The project knowledge that rarely makes it into documentation - and that decides whether you go live on time.

  • Typical MFS project phases, roles, and who owns what
  • Reading and writing telegram specifications with the equipment suppliers
  • Testing strategies - usage of emulations before the hardware exists
  • Go-live and commissioning - what the slides never tell you
What makes this different

You see the warehouse move - not just the customizing screen.

The training comes with its own MFS emulation and visualization. Two reference warehouses run live in the room: you trigger movements, watch the telegrams flow between EWM and the emulated automation, break things on purpose, and recover them with your own hands.

Pallet handling

A high-bay warehouse with conveyor loops, identification points, and crane control.

  • Pallet conveyor and crane movements end to end
  • Identification points and routing decisions
  • Exception & reject scenarios
  • Putaway and removal against the high-bay

Case / tote handling

A conveyor, sorter logic, work centers and a Miniload.

  • Tote conveyor and sortation flows
  • Work center integration and routing
  • Exception handling on the line
  • Putaway and removal against the Miniload
